The Pellegrina Viola: Where Innovation Meets Art

What is the Pellegrina?

The Pellegrina isn't just another viola - it's like a viola that's had a spa day! Often nicknamed the "Dali viola" for its artistic, almost surreal design, it's a revolutionary instrument that turns heads and changes minds. David Rivinus pays homage to Salvador Dali by incorporating the artist's signature pattern in the purfling on the back of each instrument, making every Pellegrina not just an instrument, but a piece of art. Its unique ergonomic shape sets it apart from traditional violas, allowing musicians to perform for longer periods without physical strain. Available in both standard 4-string and enhanced 5-string versions, the Pellegrina delivers the rich, warm tones of a traditional viola while offering unprecedented playing comfort.

The Story Behind the Pellegrina

The Pellegrina's journey began with David Rivinus's innovative design, driven by the desire to create a more comfortable viola without compromising sound quality. Don Tatum later developed a version with the same back length as a violin, an adaptation that Rivinus, with Tatum's permission, incorporated into his own designs. This collaborative spirit in the violin-making community led to further refinements of the instrument.

This partnership between Rivinus and Tatum showcases the power of innovation in transforming traditional instruments, proving that sometimes, two creative minds are better than one.

Playing Benefits and Sound Quality

Despite its unconventional appearance, the Pellegrina maintains the deep, resonant voice that viola players cherish. Its innovative design distributes weight more evenly across the player's body, reducing shoulder and neck strain. For professional musicians and students alike, this means longer practice sessions and performances without discomfort. The instrument's carefully crafted acoustics ensure that comfort doesn't come at the cost of sound quality.

Modern Innovation Meets Classical Tradition

Today's Pellegrina represents the perfect balance between innovation and tradition. While respecting the viola's classical heritage, it embraces modern solutions to age-old playing challenges. Musicians worldwide have discovered that this unique instrument allows them to focus more on their artistry and less on physical discomfort, making it a valuable addition to the string instrument family.
